This one is always through her eyes.

That’s how I remember it.

In this dream I’ve been out, all over the place, chasing Ripples.

Still peeling myself off the screens on the walls.

Still catching glimpses of myself through other eyes.

Blue-grey daybreak, early April.


I have a bad feeling, coming home.

Maybe I shouldn’t have left her alone tonight.

But I had to go. It was important. Why?

Maybe I shouldn’t have gotten her involved at all.

But she’s a natural. She was born to chase.

Rainboots on my feet, keycard in my hand, tired, tired.


I hope I find her curled up on the couch. Or in bed.

But such light rain can’t explain all the water in the lobby.

I take the stairs (I never take the stairs!)

At each landing, I see it leaking from behind the doors.

Not her. Please, not her.

But I already know I’m too late.


The door opens inward, only this time it doesn’t.

I push and push until finally water rushes out.

Every tap in the flat is gushing water.

Nothing behind her eyes except my own words.

The entire city is a great coral reef.

I fix her eyes. I scream her name.


“Mona!”


I blink, seeing my own face, seeing Cai Yuhui’s. And that’s when I remember who I am, where I am, what I’m doing. The water is up to my waist now. It was with such satisfaction, mere moments ago, that I kept it flowing, but now the rationale recedes with slick tentacularality into a vantablack hole in my mind. I don’t know whose thoughts those were. I don’t even know if you could call those thoughts, humming in harp-struck primary colors behind my eyes. I should have listened: I should never have looked, and especially not to impress my flatmate. Behind her, shouting, footfall, irridescent visors, vialguns. The Weather Bureau is already here.

Slowly, I take my hands off the tap, and put them in the air.
