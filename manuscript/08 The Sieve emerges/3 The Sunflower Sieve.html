Very nearly napping on a shady patch of the quad, I become distantly aware of a crowd forming nearby, of something businesslike unfolding, even of several vehicles crawling onto the sidewalks. A campus tour...no, a demonstration...an awards ceremony...a press conference? I let these possibilities float lazily past me, letting leafy spackles dancing through my eyelids explain them to me, preferring the simple heat of the sun to anything like information at this time, thank you very much. I turn towards it, unfurling myself to it, to the bees buzzing around me. I hear several voices at once in the crowd behind me repeating sunflower, sunflower, this word alone cresting above the chatter. Yes I am, thank you very much...

“— the so-called Sunflower Sieve —”

Knowing this is important to me, but too groggy to understand why, I peel myself off the ground and glare in the direction of the noise. Several news teams are on the ground, their lead engineers each huddled against a control panel, marshalling competing swarms of cameras, lights, and microphones around...who is that?...poor Dr. Guo. He keeps a level tone even as the studios flash eye-level questions in shanzi. His Mandarin is chewy; his caterpillar eyebrows track the contours of his prepared statement as though he’s reading it for the first time.

“— have not evaluated any claims of efficacy or safety —”

I dash to the clinic, already knowing what I’ll find. Three emails from different organs of YINS have already arrived, admonishing the students, faculty, and staff against using a powerful new neikotic egg that just landed online. How? Did Tethi drop the damn thing in the ruckus at Double Descent? Was it reverse-engineered by one of the quants using it? The basement hallway is so full that I can barely squeeze out of the elevator, full of neikonauts from every conceivable profession: Big Three traders and Blue Delta administrators, civil engineers and artists and computer scientists, YINS first-years huddled in horror along the wall. All of them stepping over each other to let me pass, their eyes feeling like hands, that vantablack spikiness grasping...searching...for what?
