And when I return to the Safety floor, via the tunnels, Deng and Rui are arguing. About me. I hear them coming around the bend, whispers echoing off the bare concrete. For what it’s worth, when I duck behind a large wheeled sanitation bin, it’s a fight-or-flight evasive maneuver. But from that point on — once I catch my breath — obviously I’m trying to snoop.

“— tell her that we’d take a look at it.” Deng is whispering harshly. “Are you absolutely out of your mind?”

“This arrangement can’t last, Jinghan. It’s just not fair to her. She is the one doing the work —”

They pass out of earshot, to the elevator bank. Rui calls for the upper floors, and I watch the readout tick to the fifth floor. I grab the next elevator, and follow them up. On the way, I ransack my backpack for a reason to be up here. Aha: a 48-piece puzzle of lights and prisms, the kind of soberspace projection too complicated to call an egg, overdue to the library. The doors open, and I see Deng and Rui make a right. I pace down the hall, the velvet bag of prisms clacking loudly, that was stupid — and catch them, still in heated conversation, out of earshot, somehow both shouting and whispering. Rui opens a door to a balcony just behind the stacks.

That might have been the end of my skulking. Only the windows in this part of the library are fashioned as a great matrix of primer tiles that bathe the reading room in slowly-shifting squares of color, biased orange by the early-evening sun. Knowing they can’t see me from outside, I creep through the stacks, parallel to Deng and Rui, and plant myself in the shelves, my face now plastered against the one-way window.

Through the glass, over the wind, I can’t hear a damn thing they’re saying. Rui waves his index finger out over the river, and then at himself. Deng folds her arms and fumes: not gonna happen. But I’m starting to think it has to do with...poor taste in department memorabilia. Rui produces a padded manilla envelope full of identical floppy somethings, and slides one out: it’s, duh, a sunflower. An abstract, embroidered patch: the kind you see on a space suit, or occasionally a neikosuit, if those were anything more than formalwear these days. He waves it in Deng’s face, and I can’t help but imagine him saying: I worked really hard on this!

But Deng is having none of it. She rips it from his hand and slaps him across the face with it, left-right-left. Then, furious like I’ve never seen her, she storms back inside, sending the door ricocheting. A small chorus of shhhh’s ensues. Rui stands frozen in place, and so do I, too shocked to unwedge myself from between these bookcases. Only much later do I realize I forgot to drop the bag at the returns desk.
