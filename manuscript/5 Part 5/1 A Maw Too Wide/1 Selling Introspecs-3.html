But now I flip through the readouts and see that almost every one of them is lighting up.



And up close, the ribbons are diffusing their structure into the Sea. You can see an alphabet soup of nearly transparent twisting, scintillating tile-shapes. You can hear them in the syncopations of clicks and whines that the music makes as we dance — and those rhythms seen again by the cameras, floaters in the blind eyes. The loop is closing, tightening, quickening. The thought comes staccato between heartbeats. What’s happening out there is happening in here. The implications are obscure but nooselike, with the acrid smell of unhappy coincidence, the foreclosure of possibility into claustrophobic grooves. The more we look, the more we’ll see. And the more we see, the closer we’ll look. If all of these people, regular people, are carrying traces of Sieve debris, then what could the Ripples make us see? What could they make us do?



Tethi is craning his neck above the crowd, where the Ripples are on the move. They jelly-pulse, whip-chatter, spiral-bounce, but all in the same direction. They wriggle and jostle, passing through their fellows in white-hot bursts, nearly annihilating each other in a feverish race inwards. It plays out on genzhe globes, on silkscreens descending like weeping willow, on obelisks of refurbished cell phones, as they converge from every grotto and ravine of the Mirror Sea. They follow thick, golden ribbons of Sieve debris towards something, its outline merely suggested, its gravity unmistakable.
