“It’s spreading.” Tethi gives me a weird look, a dangerous and darkly triumphant look.

“You saw this coming!” Unsure whether this merits a slap or a high five. “You did!”

“I considered it a possibility. It — would have been unwise to bring it up.”

Because he didn’t want to speak it into existence. Because — the parabola has more to say, it always does — because you caused this, Mona, you just can’t stop looking, can’t stop believing, can you?

“We need to get in there,” I tell him. I wait for some objection, but now Tethi is entranced the quasigrams overhead. The Ripples are on the move. They jelly-pulse, whip-chatter, spiral-bounce; whatever they do, it’s all in the same direction. They wriggle and jostle, passing through their fellows in white-hot bursts, practically annihilating each other in a feverish race inwards. Tethi slams his drink and returns the rollscroll to his jacket.

“I think we should follow them.”

The thick of the crowd gives me a choice: tendrils or elbows. I can look up at the Ripples and lose myself completely. That’s what everyone’s here to do: Chalker, chaser, neikonaut. Flower crowns, quasi-wands, LED-glow sunflowers raised in saucer-eyed ecstasy. Or I can keep my eyes below meridian, focus on human bodies, on arms and butts and pheromones, looking at you and you and you and sorry, excuse me, pushing through a great collective trance, seeing the wave for its particles. It becomes apparent that there are so many goddamn rooms in this place. Warehouse-sized, closet-sized, the music interpolates seamlessly between all of them. The thronging Ripples move smoothly by comparison, making it easy to believe they’re leading the dance.

One hit. Just to get our bearings. I clench my fist hard, and the autovial sends a wave of DMT into my leg. Instantly the E-flat buzz of the come-up, waves of sickly yellow and green flashing before my eyes, a sudden and elaborate gnosis. It bounces through my neikotic channels, coating them with sticky, staticky little tile-cules of Sieve debris. For the length of a terrible spasm, I have hundreds of limbs, thousands of fingers, my whole being a cartography of the Ripples’ designs on the Sea. I point, and mouth the words, but it’s so loud don’t even bother to shout: This way!

I turn around to find Tethi. Instead I see Dr. Deng Jinghan, arms crossed, taut ponytail whipping in the wind, standing tall and proud in a field of sunflowers.
