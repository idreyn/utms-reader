They wriggle and jostle, passing through their fellows in white-hot bursts, practically annihilating each other in a feverish race inwards. It plays out on genzhe globes, on silkscreens descending like weeping willow, on obelisks of refurbished cell phones, as they converge from every grotto, trench, and plain of the Mirror Sea. In the distance, they slither along thick and glittering ribbons of Sieve debris towards something dense and unseen. Up close, they, they —

Behind our eyes something stirs from a lifelong reverie, and asks: if this is me, then what am I?
