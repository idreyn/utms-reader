“Maybe they were.” But he sounds unconvinced. “Maybe they...what are you doing?”

I’m holding the rollscroll up to my face. I’m trying to see these ragged readouts with fresh eyes. That could be noise. Sensor malfunction, radio interference, anything in between. But then why do I see those spiral-claw-combs? Why is it so hard to look away? If Sunflower Sieve debris is capable of sliding into the minds of non-neikonauts, that changes the parameters of the entire experiment. I don’t have time to reprogram it now. By the way, it’s your fault, Mona. The parabola has something to add, it always does. You just can’t look away, can you?

“We need to get in there,” I insist, and wait for him to protest.

But Tethi’s barely listening. His eyes are tracing the quasigrams overhead. Some of the wild Ripples are are vast and polycentric, lurking in the saddle-points of the backscreens, feeding via interference. But most of them are moving. They jelly-pulse, whip-chatter, or spiral-bounce, but all in the same direction. They wriggle and jostle, passing through their fellows in white-hot bursts, practically annihilating each other in a feverish race inwards. Tethi slams his glass and returns the rollscroll to his jacket. “I think we should follow them.”

The thick of the crowd gives me a choice: tendrils or elbows. I can look up at the Ripples and lose myself completely. That’s what everyone’s here to do: Chalker, chaser, neikonaut. Flower crowns, quasi-wands, LED-glow sunflowers raised in saucer-eyed ecstasy. Or I can keep my eyes below meridian, focus on human bodies, on arms and butts and pheromones, looking at you and you and you and sorry, excuse me, pushing through a great collective trance, seeing the wave for its particles. It becomes apparent that there are so many goddamn rooms in this place. Warehouse-sized, closet-sized, the music interpolates seamlessly between all of them. The Ripples move so much faster than we do that it’s easy to believe they’re leading the dance.

One hit. Just to get our bearings. I clench my fist hard, and the autovial sends a wave of DMT into my leg. Instantly the E-flat buzz of the come-up, waves of sickly yellow and green flashing before my eyes, a sudden and elaborate gnosis. It bounces through my neikotic channels, coating them with sticky, staticky little tile-cules of Sieve debris. For the length of a terrible spasm, I have hundreds of limbs, thousands of fingers, my whole being a cartography of the Ripples’ designs on the Sea. I point, and mouth the words, but it’s so loud don’t even bother to shout: This way!

But when I turn around to find Tethi, I see Dr. Deng Jinghan, arms crossed, standing in a field of sunflowers.
