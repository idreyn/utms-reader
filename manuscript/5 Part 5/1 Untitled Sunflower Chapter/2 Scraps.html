The Ripples are calling their kin home.

And it plasters itself across the Mirror Sea displays until nothing else is visible, until the simple whump-whump-whump of the bassline yields to a syncopation of clicks and whines, which crescendo and flatten into a sustained and ominous drone. I’ve got it backwards. I take the measure of what’s onscreen: obscure and igneous sorrows, mantle-hot, mantle-deep. Clockwork gradients rising in elegant tiers, a frosted-glass relief map of my own soul. I stare, warren-eyed, picking my own confusion and revelation and revulsion and elation from the warm-toned patterns chattering along that ribbon of sun-soaked debris. Knots of tendrils. Behind our eyes, something stirs from a lifelong reverie. Asks: if this is me, then what am I?

Between our minds, a Ripple recognizes itself in the mirror in the Sea.
