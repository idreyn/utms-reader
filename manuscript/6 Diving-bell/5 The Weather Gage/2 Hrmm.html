Everyone can see it now, the lacework in the Sea. And no matter where you stand, it looks like it’s coming. It writhes ever-outwards, lithe and mesmerizing at scale, its wretched joints too small to see. Winding as it does, it’s hard to trace back to its source. But some see something twinkling, some deep-field spackling looking like a city on the seafloor. They point it out to their companions, who can see it too now, and so it cascades. The days are getting short; it’s twilight when Shanghai quits work for the day, and this Thursday is feeling an awful lot like a Friday. Normal people don’t check the Mirror Sea, if they do, they don’t like to talk about it. And it’s not like something new in there is, well, anything new. The psychic tectonics are constant here; eventually you don’t even react when you feel a shift. But this...but this. It’s not a Ripple, or at least not like anyone’s ever seen. It feels too mechanical.

He may not run Shanghai anymore, and he may not even run the Weather Bureau, but the two goons who do are constantly competing for his attention, and that’s all the power he ever wants again. “Zhuming, are you going to linearize him?” He waves a loose sleeve in the direction of the news. “What’s your explanation?”

On the screen, on the news, a wavefront of purple and white throbs in the margins — not the edges of the screen, but at the edge of awareness, with the general effect of inward motion. The higher-order details are bubbly, bulbous, and it only takes a second to resolve as a swarm of diving-bells. Diving-bells. Jesus Christ.
